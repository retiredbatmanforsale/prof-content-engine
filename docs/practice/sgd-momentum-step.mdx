import numpy as np

def sgd_momentum_step(params, grads, velocities, lr, momentum):
    for p, g, v in zip(params, grads, velocities):
        # v *= momentum then v += g, in place.
        v *= momentum
        v += g
        p -= lr * v
`}

The simplest optimiser upgrade after vanilla SGD — and still the dominant choice for ConvNets. Adam and AdamW have largely replaced it for transformer training, but momentum is the foundation both build on.

The "heavy-ball" intuition: instead of stepping in the gradient direction, accumulate a velocity that smooths over recent gradients. Steady descents speed up; stochastic noise averages out.

Update rule (PyTorch convention):

`v ← momentum · v + g`

`p ← p − lr · v`

Two subtleties the harness probes:

1. **Velocity convergence.** With a constant gradient `g` and no parameter updates (set `lr=0`), the velocity update is a geometric series — it converges to `g / (1 - momentum)`. For `momentum=0.9`, that's `10·g`. Test `iv-2` verifies this — get the formula wrong and the limit will be off.
2. **In-place mutation of BOTH params and velocities.** Velocity buffers persist across training steps; if you create new arrays each step, the optimiser is effectively SGD without memory.

Typical setting: `lr=0.01, momentum=0.9` for ResNet-style CNNs. Not used directly for transformers (which prefer AdamW), but every implementation of SGD+momentum starts from this exact two-line update.
