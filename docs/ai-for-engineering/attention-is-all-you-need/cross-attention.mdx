Students often jumble three ideas: **self-attention**, **multi-head attention**, and **cross-attention**. They are **not** three unrelated tricks—**multi-head** is a **way to run** attention (several heads in parallel); **self** vs **cross** describes **where Q, K, V come from**. **Cross-attention** is the variant where **queries** are built from **one** sequence (the **decoder**) and **keys** and **values** from **another** (the **encoder**). The **math** is the **same** scaled dot-product rule; only the **sources** of the three matrices change.

So: **multi-head** answers “**how many** parallel attention maps?” **Self vs cross** answers “**which tensors** built **Q, K, V**?”

## Cross-attention: where **Q**, **K**, **V** come from

Let **H**^dec be the decoder hidden states (target side, shape **(batch, target_len, d_model)**) and **H**^enc be the encoder output (source side, **(batch, source_len, d_model)**). Learned projections:

$$
Q = H^{\text{dec}} W^Q,\qquad K = H^{\text{enc}} W^K,\qquad V = H^{\text{enc}} W^V
$$

- **Query Q** — “What am I looking for **right now** on the target?” — from the **decoder**.
- **Key K** — “How do **source** positions label themselves for matching?” — from the **encoder**.
- **Value V** — “What **content** do I retrieve from the **source**?” — from the **encoder**.

Compact mnemonic (as in your notes and the [Lex AI](https://aiayn.aiseekhegaindia.com/) cross-attention section):

## The formula does **not** change

Scaled dot-product attention is **unchanged**:

$$
\text{CrossAttention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{Q K^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right) V
$$

- **Q** has rows indexed by **target** positions (queries).
- **K**, **V** have rows indexed by **source** positions.
- **QKᵀ** has shape **(target_len, source_len)**: each target position assigns a **weight** to **every** source token.
- **Softmax** is over the **source** axis → a **distribution** over the input sentence for **each** decoding step.
- Multiplying by **V** **mixes** encoder vectors according to those weights.

So: **same** **softmax(QKᵀ / √d_k) V**; **different** **construction** of **Q** vs **K, V** compared to self-attention.

## Why cross-attention matters (encoder–decoder & machine translation)

In **seq2seq** **machine translation**:

1. The **encoder** reads the **source** (e.g. English) **in full** and produces a **sequence of memory vectors**.
2. The **decoder** generates the **target** (e.g. French) **one token at a time**, with **causal** self-attention on the target.
3. **Cross-attention** is the **only** place the decoder **reads** the **encoder**: it **chooses** which **source** words (or subwords) to emphasize when producing the **next** target token.

Without cross-attention, the decoder would be a **language model** on the target only—it could not **condition** on the **specific source** sentence. Cross-attention is **necessary** for the **original** Transformer **translation** setup and for any **encoder–decoder** task where the output must **align** with a **given** input (translation, some summarization setups, speech-to-text stacks, etc.).

**Alignment:** attention weights between target step and source positions are often plotted as **heatmaps**—early papers showed interpretable **word alignment** patterns.

---

## What does **not** use cross-attention?

**Decoder-only** models (**GPT** family) have **no** encoder: they use **masked self-attention** + **FFN** only. **Encoder-only** models (**BERT**) use **self-attention** only. **Cross-attention** appears when **two** representations must **interact**—**decoder** × **encoder**.

---

## Multi-head cross-attention

In practice, cross-attention is **multi-head** just like self-attention: **h** sets of **(Q_h, K_h, V_h)** with **Q_h** from decoder and **K_h, V_h** from encoder, then **concat** and **W^O**. The **distinction** (cross vs self) is unchanged—only the **tensor sources**.

---

## Takeaway

| Question | Answer |
| -------- | ------ |
| **Q from?** | **Decoder** hidden states |
| **K, V from?** | **Encoder** outputs |
| **Formula?** | **Identical** to self-attention: **softmax(QKᵀ/√d_k) V** |
| **Why in MT?** | Lets each **target** step **attend** to **source** memory—**conditional** generation |
