The paper is titled **“Attention Is All You Need”**—and attention **is** the mechanism that **routes** information between tokens. But every transformer layer in [Vaswani et al. (2017)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762) also contains a **position-wise feed-forward network (FFN)**. **Attention** answers *who to read*; the **FFN** answers *how to process what you read* at **each position** with **nonlinearity** and **high capacity**. Without the FFN, the block would be missing half of what makes deep transformers **expressive**.

  <p className="m-0 text-sm text-slate-800 dark:text-slate-200">
    The full layer diagram on [Attention is All You Need — Lex AI](https://aiayn.aiseekhegaindia.com/) shows the same pattern: <strong>Attention</strong> (or masked / cross) → <strong>Add &amp; Norm</strong> → <strong>Feed-Forward</strong> → <strong>Add &amp; Norm</strong>—in both <strong>encoder</strong> and <strong>decoder</strong> stacks, and in <strong>decoder-only</strong> models that drop the encoder entirely.
  </p>
</div>

## The paper’s definition (ReLU)

Each position gets the **same** two-layer MLP applied to its vector **x** ∈ ℝᵈᵐᵒᵈᵉˡ:

$$
\text{FFN}(x) = \max\bigl(0,\, x W_1 + b_1\bigr) W_2 + b_2
$$

Here **max(0, ·)** is **ReLU**. **W₁** maps **d_model → d_ff**, **W₂** maps **d_ff → d_model**. In the baseline setup, **d_ff = 2048** when **d_model = 512**—about **four times** wider—then **back** to model width.

<div className="not-prose my-6 rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-300 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-sky-50 p-5 dark:border-emerald-800 dark:from-emerald-950/30 dark:to-sky-950/30">
  <p className="m-0 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-widest text-emerald-800 dark:text-emerald-300">Position-wise · same weights · every token</p>
  <p className="mt-3 mb-0 text-center text-sm text-slate-700 dark:text-slate-300">
    For sequence matrix <strong>X</strong> with shape <strong>(batch, seq, d_model)</strong>, the FFN is applied <strong>row-wise</strong>—<strong>no</strong> mixing across time inside the FFN. Mixing is attention’s job.
  </p>
</div>

**Modern variants** often swap ReLU for **GELU** or other activations; the **shape** of the block stays: **expand → nonlinear → contract**.

## Why do we need FFN at all?

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-x-auto rounded-xl border border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-700">
  <table className="w-full min-w-[300px] border-collapse text-left text-sm">
    <thead>
      <tr className="bg-slate-700 text-white dark:bg-slate-900">
        <th className="p-3 font-semibold w-[34%]">Attention does…</th>
        <th className="p-3 font-semibold">FFN adds…</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody className="text-slate-800 dark:text-slate-200">
      <tr className="border-b border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-700">
        <td className="p-3">**Mixes** vectors **across positions** (weighted sum of **values**)</td>
        <td className="p-3">**Processes** each position **alone** after mixing</td>
      </tr>
      <tr className="border-b border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-700">
        <td className="p-3">With fixed weights, linear in **V** (softmax nonlin on **scores** only)</td>
        <td className="p-3">**Pointwise nonlinearity** (ReLU/GELU) on **features**—essential **depth** of computation per token</td>
      </tr>
      <tr className="border-b border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-700">
        <td className="p-3">Shared **d_model** channel for all heads’ output</td>
        <td className="p-3">**d_ff &gt; d_model** “**thinking**” space before projecting back</td>
      </tr>
      <tr className="bg-violet-50/50 dark:bg-violet-950/20">
        <td className="p-3" colSpan={2}>
          **Rhythm of a layer:** <strong>Attention</strong> = collect context from elsewhere → <strong>FFN</strong> = integrate and transform **locally**. Stack **L** layers → **L** rounds of **global mix** + **local nonlinear** refine.
        </td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

So: attention is **not** “all you need” for a **single** layer’s full expressive power—the **FFN** is the standard place for **heavy** per-token computation. The **title** means recurrence/convolution are **not** required for **sequence routing**; **FFN** is still required in the **original** design (and in **GPT**/**BERT**-style stacks) as the **co-equal** half of each block.

---

## Encoder, decoder, decoder-only

| Stack | FFN after… | Same FFN idea? |
| ----- | ---------- | -------------- |
| **Encoder** | Multi-head **self-attention** | **Yes** — position-wise MLP on each position |
| **Decoder** | Masked self-attention, then **cross-attention** | **Yes** — FFN after the **cross-attention** sublayer (per paper stack) |
| **Decoder-only (e.g. GPT)** | Causal self-attention only | **Yes** — still **two** sublayers per block: attention + **FFN** |

The **weights** of the FFN are **not** shared with attention; they are **separate** parameters that **specialize** for **feature processing** after attention has **rewired** context.

---

## Parameters (why **d_ff** is large)

Roughly, two matrices of size **d_model × d_ff** and **d_ff × d_model** dominate the FFN—**O(d_model · d_ff)** parameters per layer. That is often **most** of a layer’s parameter count alongside attention projections. The **width** **d_ff** is a **knob** for **capacity** vs **cost**.

## Takeaway

- **FFN(x) = max(0, xW₁ + b₁)W₂ + b₂** in the paper (**ReLU**); same **position-wise** block appears **after** attention in **encoder–decoder** and **decoder-only** transformers.
- **Value added:** **nonlinearity**, **high-width** computation per token, and **alternation** with attention so each layer both **gathers** context and **thinks** locally.
- Together with **residuals** and **LayerNorm**, the FFN makes **deep** stacks **trainable** and **expressive**.
