**Attention** selects **what to read** from a set of vectors. You have a **query** (“what am I looking for?”) and a collection of **keys** and **values** (“what is available?” / “what do I get if I pick this?”). A **similarity** between query and keys yields **weights**; the output is a **weighted sum** of values.

## Scaled dot-product attention

For one query vector **q** and key–value pairs **(kⱼ, vⱼ)**:

$$
\text{Attention}(q, K, V) = \sum_j \underbrace{\frac{\exp(q \cdot k_j / \sqrt{d_k})}{\sum_{j'} \exp(q \cdot k_{j'} / \sqrt{d_k})}}_{\text{softmax weights}} v_j
$$

In matrix form (batch of queries):

$$
\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right) V
$$

The **√d_k** scale keeps dot products from growing too large as dimension increases, so softmax stays **sharp but not saturated**.

---

## Intuition

- High **q · k** → large weight on that **v**.
- Softmax → weights are **positive** and **sum to 1** (convex combination of values).

**Next:** when **Q, K, V** all come from the **same** sequence, you get <strong>self-attention</strong>.
