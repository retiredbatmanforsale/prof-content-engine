## Process overview {#process-overview}

The Apple ICT2 loop runs **4–8 weeks** end-to-end:

1. **Recruiter screen** — 20–30 min: background, motivation, Apple interest, salary
2. **Hiring manager screen** — often more technical than at peer companies; deep dive on past projects
3. **Technical phone screen** — 45–60 min on CoderPad, 1–2 coding problems
4. **Onsite loop** — 4–5 rounds, ~45–60 min each, ~4–5 hours total. Mix of:
   - 2 coding rounds (LC-style with Apple twists)
   - 1 feature-level design (NOT distributed systems at ICT2)
   - 1 behavioral
   - 1 domain-specific (iOS / macOS frameworks, team-flavored)
5. **Decision** — team consensus + leveling discussion. Apple\'s leveling is famously opaque; offers can come at ICT2 or sometimes ICT3 depending on the team\'s read.

:::warning[There is no "the Apple interview"]
Apple lets candidates interview with **multiple teams in parallel**. Each team designs its own loop. Your friend\'s Apple interview is not your Apple interview. Confirm with your recruiter what the specific team\'s format will be.
:::

## Apple leveling explained {#apple-leveling}

| Level | Title | Years exp | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ICT2** | SWE | 0–2 | **This guide — entry / new grad** |
| ICT3 | SWE | 2–5 | Mid-level |
| ICT4 | Senior SWE | 5+ | Senior |
| ICT5 | Staff SWE | 8+ | Staff |
| ICT6 | Principal | 10+ | Principal |

ICT2 is where Apple typically hires new grads. The expectation: execute well-defined tasks under mentorship, with strong fundamentals and ramp-up potential. You\'re not expected to own a whole feature yet — that\'s ICT3+.

## Why Apple loops vary by team {#team-specific-nature}

Apple\'s engineering org is structured around products and platforms (iOS, macOS, Photos, Maps, Health, etc.). Each team has substantial autonomy in interview design. As a result:

- **Languages vary.** Apple-platform teams often expect Swift or Objective-C. Services and ML teams may lean Python / C++.
- **System design questions vary.** Most ICT2 design questions are **feature-level** ("design Recently Deleted in Photos") rather than distributed systems.
- **Behavioral focus varies.** UI-heavy teams probe pixel-perfect mindset and designer collaboration; ML teams probe privacy and on-device constraints; infra teams lean closer to standard SWE behavioral.

Ask your recruiter explicitly: *"What language is preferred? What kind of design problems? What does the loop look like?"* They will tell you.

## Technical phone screen {#technical-phone-screen}

One or two medium coding problems with follow-ups. Apple frequently adds an Apple-platform twist to standard problems:

- "Implement an LRU cache that handles concurrent access via GCD."
- "Solve this problem with attention to memory — what\'s the heap allocation pattern?"
- "Now imagine this needs to run on-device with 1MB memory budget."

The interviewer also asks 2–3 ecosystem questions: do you understand reference counting in Swift, value vs. reference types, Foundation collections vs. Swift native types, async-await semantics.

## Onsite (4–5 rounds) {#onsite}

<InterviewRound name="Coding 1" duration="60 min" format="Live coding · whiteboard or shared editor" weight="signal">

LeetCode-style medium with Apple-specific twists. Common categories: trees, graphs, strings, recursion, intervals.

The interviewer often adds a follow-up about real-world Apple product optimization: "How would you make this fast enough for the Photos app to use it on a 100k-image library?"

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="Coding 2" duration="60 min" format="Live coding · whiteboard or shared editor" weight="signal">

Same format, different interviewer, harder problem. Often emphasizes:

- **Algorithm optimization** — moving from naive O(n²) to O(n log n) when memory matters
- **Code cleanliness** — modularity, testability, naming
- **Edge cases** — Apple is unusually rigorous here; expect probes on null, empty, max-bounds

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="Feature-Level Design" duration="45 min" format="Whiteboard / virtual canvas" weight="signal">

**Not distributed systems** at ICT2. Apple\'s ICT2 design questions stay at the feature level:

- Design the "Recently Deleted" album in Photos (with restore + auto-purge)
- Design offline mode for Notes app with iCloud sync
- Design a privacy-respecting health-data export feature
- Design notification grouping for Messages
- Design a media picker that respects on-device privacy

Expected flow:

1. **Clarify product requirements** (5 min) — what does the user actually do? what\'s the success criterion?
2. **Data model** (5–10 min) — entities, fields, relationships.
3. **API / interface** (5–10 min) — public surface, edge cases.
4. **Storage and sync** (10 min) — on-device vs. iCloud, what\'s persisted, what\'s ephemeral.
5. **Privacy considerations** (5 min) — what data leaves the device? what\'s encrypted? what does the user explicitly consent to?

<InterviewRound name="Behavioral" duration="45 min" format="Conversational" weight="signal">

Apple\'s behavioral round is unusually focused on **culture fit**:

- **Designer / engineer collaboration** — how you handled disagreement with a designer or PM
- **Pixel-perfect mindset** — caring about details others might dismiss as minor
- **Privacy-first thinking** — choices you made about data collection or handling
- **Attention to detail** — a time you caught something subtle that mattered
- **Long product cycles** — comfort with shipping seasonally rather than weekly
- **Why Apple specifically** — concrete answers about products you use and care about

A common Apple red flag: **shipping for shipping\'s sake**. If your stories are all about velocity without quality concern, the interviewer flags it.

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="Domain-Specific (team-flavored)" duration="45 min" format="Conversational + light technical" weight="signal">

The team-specific round. Content depends entirely on the team:

- **iOS / macOS app teams** — UIKit / SwiftUI, Auto Layout, view lifecycles, Core Data
- **Frameworks teams** — system frameworks, low-level concurrency, public API design
- **ML / Siri teams** — Core ML, on-device inference, model compression
- **Services / cloud teams** — server-side Swift, iCloud architecture
- **Hardware-adjacent teams** — driver layers, embedded constraints, battery profiling

Ask your recruiter what to expect — they will tell you specifically which framework or domain the round will cover.

</InterviewRound>

## Apple-specific themes {#apple-themes}

Across all rounds, these themes recur and are **scored**, not just discussed:

1. **Privacy by default.** "How would you handle user data securely in this feature?" is asked at every level. Defaults that minimize data collection score better than features with elaborate protections after the fact.
2. **On-device processing.** Apple has a strategic preference for local computation over cloud dependency. Designs that work offline and don\'t leak data score better.
3. **Resource constraints.** Battery, bandwidth, storage, and memory are first-class concerns. ICT2 candidates are expected to reason about these.
4. **Swift / GCD literacy** for platform-adjacent teams. Threading discipline matters; UI thread vs. background work is a real signal.
5. **Pixel-perfect quality.** Apple explicitly hires for taste. Your behavioral stories should reflect this.

## Preparation plan {#preparation-plan}

| Week | Focus | Daily commitment | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | LC mediums (Apple-tagged: trees, graphs, strings, recursion) | 2 mediums/day | 14 mediums solved cleanly |
| 2 | LC mediums + memory-aware optimization | 2 mediums/day with allocation analysis | All mediums solved with memory discussion |
| 3 | Swift / GCD / async-await fundamentals (if platform team) | 1 hour/day | Comfortable with concurrency primitives, value vs reference types, retain cycles |
| 4 | Feature-level design reps (Photos, Notes, Messages, Health) | 1 design/day | 5 feature designs with privacy and on-device reasoning |
| 5 | Apple-cultural behavioral story building (5 stories) | 30 min/day + 1 mock | 5 STAR stories: quality, privacy, designer collaboration, attention to detail, "why Apple" |
| 6 | Mock loops + team-specific prep (whatever your recruiter named) | 1 mock loop | 2 full mocks, including one team-flavored round |

:::tip[Apple-specific gotchas]
**"Why Apple?"** is asked unironically and weighted. Generic answers ("influence at scale," "great products") flag. A concrete answer like *"I\'ve used Notes daily for 8 years and I\'m fascinated by how the iCloud sync handles offline edits — I want to work on the substrate that makes this feel reliable"* lands.

**Privacy-by-default reasoning is scored.** Even if the team isn\'t privacy-adjacent, candidates who raise privacy considerations naturally score better. Practice incorporating it as a default, not as a checkbox.

**Use the recruiter.** Apple recruiters are unusually informative about their team\'s specific format. Ask explicitly what language, what kind of design questions, what behavioral themes — they\'ll tell you.
:::
