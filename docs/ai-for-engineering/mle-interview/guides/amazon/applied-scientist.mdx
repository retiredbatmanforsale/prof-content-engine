## Process overview {#process-overview}

The Amazon Applied Scientist loop runs **6–10 weeks** end-to-end:

1. **Recruiter screen** — 30 min logistics + light technical fit
2. **Technical phone screen** — 60 min: ML + coding + LP. (1–2 screens depending on role and seniority)
3. **Onsite loop** — 5 rounds, 55 min each:
   - Coding (1–2 rounds)
   - ML Breadth
   - ML Depth (research project deep-dive)
   - ML Application (model design for a business scenario)
   - Leadership / Behavioral
   - Bar Raiser
4. **Tech talk** (some teams) — 60 min research presentation to the science community

:::warning[LP signal everywhere]
Leadership Principles are tested **in every round**, not just the behavioral round. Each interviewer asks 2–3 LP questions in addition to the technical content. Recent reports indicate **>50% of total interview time is behavioral**.
:::

## How it differs from the SDE ladder {#how-it-differs-from-sde}

Applied Scientist is a **separate job family** from SDE at Amazon, with its own leveling and rubric:

| Dimension | SDE ladder | Applied Scientist ladder |
|---|---|---|
| **Coding emphasis** | High (2-3 dedicated rounds) | Moderate (1-2 rounds, mediums) |
| **System design** | Mandatory at L5+ | Replaced by ML application + research talk |
| **ML depth** | Not assessed | Required — your published or industry research |
| **ML breadth** | Not assessed | Required — fundamentals across supervised, unsupervised, deep learning |
| **Research talk** | Not part of loop | Often required (1-hour presentation) |
| **PhD relevance** | Not weighted | Weighted (especially for L5+ AS roles), but industry research counts |

## Technical phone screen {#technical-phone-screen}

<InterviewRound
  name="Technical Phone Screen"
  duration="60 min"
  format="Video · 1–2 sessions · senior team member"
  weight="gate"
>

Mixed bag: 1–2 coding problems + ML fundamentals + 1–2 LP questions. The first screen is typically:

- **20–25 min coding** — DSA medium (arrays, hashmaps, graphs, trees, BST). Not the level of LeetCode-hard you\'d see for an SDE.
- **20 min ML** — fundamentals only on this round: bias-variance, regularization, loss functions, evaluation metrics. The interviewer probes whether your ML grounding is solid before scheduling onsite.
- **10–15 min LP** — 1–2 questions, drilled lightly.

If a second phone screen exists, it usually goes deeper on ML (your research, model design tradeoffs).

</InterviewRound>

## Onsite (5 rounds) {#onsite}

<InterviewRound
  name="Coding"
  duration="55 min · 1–2 rounds depending on team"
  format="CoderPad / shared editor"
  weight="gate"
>

LeetCode-style DSA problems at medium level — arrays, hashmaps, graphs, trees, BFS/DFS, sliding window, binary search, basic DP. The bar is "can you write working code under pressure" rather than "can you crack the hardest problem."

The interviewer also asks 2–3 LP questions in this round (15 min). Don\'t treat coding rounds as pure technical.

<InterviewRound
  name="ML Breadth"
  duration="55 min"
  format="Conversational + whiteboard"
  weight="signal"
>

Tests the **width** of your ML knowledge across topics. Common probes:

- **Supervised learning** — linear/logistic regression, decision trees, random forests, GBMs (XGBoost, LightGBM), SVMs, neural networks
- **Unsupervised** — k-means, GMM, DBSCAN, hierarchical clustering, anomaly detection
- **Probabilistic models** — naive Bayes, HMMs, Bayesian inference basics
- **Deep learning** — CNNs, RNNs, attention/Transformers, optimization, regularization (dropout, BatchNorm, weight decay)
- **NLP / GenAI** — embeddings, fine-tuning, RAG, evaluation of LLMs
- **Methodology** — bias-variance, regularization, cross-validation, hyperparameter tuning, evaluation metrics for various tasks

The interviewer rapidly switches topics. You\'re not expected to be expert at everything — but you must be able to **discuss tradeoffs and pick the right tool** for a given problem.

<InterviewRound
  name="ML Depth — Research Deep-Dive"
  duration="55 min"
  format="Walkthrough of your past project · whiteboard for derivations"
  weight="level"
>

You walk through a **real research or applied ML project** you\'ve owned (paper, internship work, side project). The interviewer drills into:

- **Problem framing** — why this problem? what was the metric? what counted as success?
- **Data** — how was it collected? what biases were there? how did you handle them?
- **Method** — why this model? what alternatives did you consider? what did you implement vs. use off-the-shelf?
- **Tradeoffs** — what did you give up? what would you do differently?
- **Outcomes** — what worked, what didn\'t, what did you learn?
- **Math** — derivations and intuitions for the key components (be ready to whiteboard a loss function or attention mechanism)

This is the round that **decides level**. Strong depth on a real project — even an unpublished one — beats shallow familiarity with several papers. Have **one project rehearsed cold** at 4 levels of follow-up.

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound
  name="ML Application"
  duration="55 min"
  format="Whiteboard · open-ended business scenario"
  weight="level"
>

The interviewer presents a real Amazon-flavored business problem and asks you to design the ML system end-to-end. Examples:

- "Design a system to detect fraudulent reviews on Amazon."
- "Design a recommendation engine for Prime Video."
- "Design a query understanding system for Amazon search."
- "Design a forecasting system for inventory at Amazon Fresh."
- "Design a moderation system for Twitch using a multimodal LLM."

Expected flow:

1. **Problem framing** (5–10 min) — clarify objectives, success metrics, constraints (latency, scale, cost).
2. **Data design** (10 min) — what data, how to collect it, what labels, how to handle biases.
3. **Model architecture** (15 min) — start simple, add complexity only when motivated. Include training and inference pipelines.
4. **Eval strategy** (5 min) — offline metrics, online experimentation (A/B), guardrails.
5. **Deployment + monitoring** (10 min) — serving infra, latency budgets, retraining cadence, drift detection.
6. **Failure modes** (5 min) — what breaks at scale, what biases creep in, how to debug.

This round mirrors **what an Applied Scientist actually does at Amazon** — taking a vague business problem and shipping a model that works in production.

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound
  name="Behavioral / Leadership Principles"
  duration="55 min"
  format="Conversational"
  weight="signal"
>

Pure LP round (the other rounds also test LPs, but this is the dedicated one). At the Applied Scientist level, the most-probed principles:

- **Customer Obsession** — choosing the user-aligned model over the academically-clean one
- **Earn Trust** — communicating model risk and uncertainty to non-technical stakeholders
- **Dive Deep** — debugging a model failure, understanding all layers of your pipeline
- **Are Right, A Lot** — judgment under data ambiguity, when to ship vs. iterate
- **Bias for Action** — making 2-way-door experiments quickly
- **Invent and Simplify** — replacing a complex model with something simpler that works as well

Have **10–12 STAR stories** prepared, mapped to LPs.

</InterviewRound>

## The Bar Raiser {#bar-raiser}

The Bar Raiser at Applied Scientist is from outside your target science team but inside the broader Amazon science org. They probe:

- **Whether you raise the bar of current Applied Scientists** in research depth and applied judgment
- **Whether your ML breadth is wide enough** to support the team\'s long-term work
- **Whether your judgment under ambiguity** is sound — would they trust your model decisions

The Bar Raiser veto is binding here too. A common pattern: strong ML depth + weak ML breadth gets a downlevel ("we\'d like to extend at L4 instead of L5").

## Preparation plan {#preparation-plan}

| Week | Focus | Daily commitment | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | LC mediums (graphs, trees, BFS/DFS, DP) | 2 mediums/day | 14 mediums solved cleanly |
| 2 | ML breadth review (supervised, unsupervised, deep learning fundamentals) | 1 topic/day with whiteboard derivations | All listed topics with tradeoff statements |
| 3 | ML depth — pick your strongest project, rehearse 4 levels deep | 1 hour rehearsal + 1 mock | Project survives 4-level drill-down without collapse |
| 4 | ML application reps (5 end-to-end designs) | 1 design/day | 5 designs: fraud, rec sys, forecasting, moderation, query understanding |
| 5 | Tech talk preparation (if your role requires it) | 30 min/day | 60-min talk delivered cleanly with Q&A buffer |
| 6 | LP story building (10–12 stories) | 30 min stories + 1 mock LP | 10 stories with metrics, mapped to LPs |
| 7 | Mock loops (full or near-full) | 1 mock loop/week | 2 full mocks with no flagged round |

:::tip[The most common mistake]
Strong-depth-only candidates fail. **You can have one excellent project and still tank the breadth round.** Spend Week 2 forcing yourself to discuss every ML topic area, not just your specialty.
:::
