## Process overview {#process-overview}

The Google L3 loop runs **6–10 weeks**:

1. **Recruiter screen** — 30 min, logistics + light technical fit.
2. **Technical phone screen** — 45 min, 1–2 coding problems.
3. **Onsite (virtual loop)** — 4 rounds, usually one day:
   - 2 coding rounds
   - 1 "Googleyness & leadership" (behavioral)
   - 1 ML domain round (for MLE roles) **or** an additional coding round (for generalist SWE).
4. **Hiring Committee review** — your packet goes to a committee that decides hire/no-hire **without** meeting you.
5. **Team match** — if hired, you're matched to a team after committee approval.

:::warning[Different from Meta]
Google does **not** match you to a team during the loop. You can pass the bar and still wait weeks for a team match. Don't optimize prep around a specific team — optimize for committee signal.
:::

## Technical phone screen {#technical-phone-screen}

<InterviewRound
  name="Technical Phone Screen"
  duration="45 min"

Google still conducts the phone screen on **a shared Google Doc**, not an IDE. You will not have syntax highlighting or a compiler. Practice on plain text before this round — it is genuinely different from CoderPad.

The interviewer is calibrating against the same rubric used in the onsite, so prepare it as the same round.

## Onsite (4 rounds) {#onsite}

<InterviewRound name="Coding 1 — Data Structures & Algorithms" duration="45 min" format="Google Docs" weight="signal">
One main problem with 1–2 follow-ups. Common: trees, graphs, recursion, DP, intervals. The follow-up usually generalizes the original problem (e.g., "now do it with k arrays instead of 2").
</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="Coding 2 — Implementation" duration="45 min" format="Google Docs" weight="signal">
Slightly more implementation-heavy than Coding 1 — string parsing, simulation, or a system component (e.g., LRU cache, rate limiter). The interviewer cares more about correctness and edge-case discipline than raw algorithmic insight.
</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="ML Domain (MLE roles only)" duration="45 min" format="Conversational + whiteboard" weight="level">

For MLE candidates, this replaces one of the coding rounds. The interviewer probes:

- **Fundamentals** — bias-variance, regularization, evaluation metrics for the candidate's chosen problem (classification vs. ranking vs. recommendation).
- **A practical problem** — "design the model and training pipeline for X" (e.g., spam classifier, news ranker).
- **Recent reading** — what models or papers are you tracking in your area?

<EvalCriteria
  criteria={[
    { name: 'Concept clarity', detail: 'You can explain why log loss is preferred to MSE for classification, what regularization does, and what overfitting looks like — without hand-waving.' },
    { name: 'Pipeline thinking', detail: 'You design the full pipeline (data, features, model, eval, monitoring), not just "I\'d use XGBoost."' },
    { name: 'GenAI literacy', detail: 'You can discuss when to use an LLM vs. a classical model, and the cost/latency/accuracy tradeoff.', strong: 'You\'ve hands-on built a non-trivial RAG or fine-tuning project and can describe what failed.' },
  ]}
/>

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="Googleyness & Leadership" duration="45 min" format="Conversational" weight="signal">

Google's behavioral round is lighter than Meta's "Jedi" but still a real signal. The interviewer probes:

- **Comfort with ambiguity** — situations where requirements were unclear and what you did.
- **Collaboration** — feedback culture, how you handle disagreements.
- **Bias toward action** — when you took initiative without being asked.

For L3, examples can be from internships, school projects, or early-career work. Scale of impact is **not** the signal — clarity of self-reflection is.

</InterviewRound>

## Hiring Committee {#hiring-committee}

After your onsite, your interviewers' written feedback goes to a Hiring Committee — a group of 4–6 senior engineers from across Google who haven't met you. They vote hire/no-hire based on the **packet alone**.

This means:

- **Interviewer write-up quality matters as much as your performance.** Speak clearly enough that your interviewer can write strong evidence into the packet — vague answers produce vague write-ups.
- **A weak signal cannot be saved by manager advocacy.** Unlike many companies, your future manager has no influence over the hire decision.

## Preparation plan {#preparation-plan}

| Week | Focus | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | LC mediums + Google Doc practice (no IDE) | 25 mediums solved on plain text |
| 3 | LC hards (selectively — 8–10 of the most common) | Solve LC 4, 42, 23 cleanly |
| 4 | ML fundamentals refresher (for MLE roles) | Re-derive logistic regression, explain bias-variance |
| 5 | ML system design reps (5 walkthroughs) | Spam classifier, ranker, recommender, search, fraud detection |
| 6 | Behavioral + 2 mocks | 3 STAR stories, 2 full mocks |
