## Process overview {#process-overview}

The Microsoft L63 (Senior SDE) loop runs **4–8 weeks** end-to-end:

1. **Recruiter / Hiring Manager screen** — 30 min
2. **Coding assessment** (HackerRank) — 90 min, 2 problems (1 approachable, 1 challenging) — *or* **Technical phone screen** (60 min, 2–3 LeetCode-style problems live)
3. **Onsite loop** — 4–5 rounds, mostly virtual via Teams:
   - 2–3 DSA coding rounds
   - 1 LLD (low-level design) round
   - 1 system design round
   - Behavioral woven into each technical round (no standalone round at L63)
4. **As-Appropriate (As-Ap)** — invitation-only finale if earlier rounds went well

:::warning[Where L63 is decided]
At L63, **system design is the level-deciding round** — strong coding will not lift you over the bar without it. The LLD round is where Microsoft tests whether you can implement *and* design at senior depth. Behavioral matters but no single round decides it; the cumulative signal across rounds counts.
:::

## How it differs from L62 {#how-it-differs-from-l62}

| Dimension | L62 (mid SDE II) | L63 (Senior SDE) |
|---|---|---|
| **System design** | Required, but high-level breadth | **Deeper** — interviewer drills 3+ levels into one component |
| **LLD round** | Often part of coding | Frequently a dedicated 60-min round |
| **Coding bar** | Mediums, occasional medium-hard | Mediums + medium-hard, with implementation depth |
| **Behavioral expectation** | Growth mindset, collaboration | Cross-team mentorship, technical leadership, strategic judgment |
| **Ambiguity handling** | Asks clarifying questions | "Make reasonable assumptions" without getting blocked |
| **Stakeholder scope** | Within-team | Cross-org, including PM/design partner work |

## Online assessment {#online-assessment}

<InterviewRound
  name="Coding Assessment (HackerRank)"
  duration="90 min"
  format="HackerRank · 2 problems · no retakes"
  weight="gate"
>

Two problems on HackerRank. Common pattern: one approachable medium and one harder medium with non-trivial follow-ups. Topics: arrays, strings, hash maps, trees, graphs, BFS/DFS, sliding window, basic DP.

**No retakes.** A weak performance here ends the loop before the onsite is scheduled.

</InterviewRound>

## Technical phone screen {#technical-phone-screen}

<InterviewRound
  name="Technical Phone Screen"
  duration="60 min"
  format="Live with engineer · 2-3 problems · CodingPad"
  weight="gate"
>

Sometimes substitutes for the OA, sometimes added on top. 2–3 LeetCode-style problems with follow-ups. The interviewer also asks 1–2 behavioral questions.

</InterviewRound>

## Onsite (4–5 rounds) {#onsite}

<InterviewRound name="Coding 1 — DSA" duration="45–60 min" format="Collaborative editor" weight="signal">

One medium-hard problem with follow-ups. Common: trees, graphs, advanced DP, intervals, design problems (LRU cache, serialize/deserialize tree). Recently asked: Integer to English Words (LC 273), LRU Cache (LC 146), Serialize/Deserialize Binary Tree (LC 297), Spiral Matrix (LC 54).

The interviewer also probes a predetermined behavioral focus area (you\'ll be told beforehand which area — e.g., "growth mindset," "feedback handling," "cross-team collaboration").

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="Coding 2 — DSA" duration="45–60 min" format="Collaborative editor" weight="signal">

Same format, different interviewer, different behavioral focus area. Often skews toward implementation discipline rather than pure algorithmic insight.

</InterviewRound>

<InterviewRound name="Low-Level Design (LLD)" duration="60 min" format="Whiteboard / virtual canvas" weight="signal">

Often a dedicated round at L63 (sometimes embedded in coding at L62). The interviewer asks you to design and partially implement a complex component:

- Design a logger framework (multi-level, async, file rotation)
- Design a parking lot with concurrency
- Design a job scheduler with priority and retries
- Design a chess game engine
- Design a distributed cache (interface + key methods)

Expected: classes with relationships, public APIs, key methods implemented in code, edge cases (concurrency, eviction, retries) explicitly handled.

<InterviewRound name="System Design (HLD)" duration="60 min" format="Whiteboard discussion" weight="level">

The level-deciding round. Microsoft asks for **first-principles** distributed system design, often around Microsoft products:

- Design OneDrive\'s file sync at billions-of-files scale
- Design Teams chat with persistence + presence + reactions
- Design Azure Blob Storage with strong consistency guarantees
- Design Outlook with calendar availability search across millions of mailboxes
- Design SharePoint Online with cross-tenant document sharing
- Design a distributed rate limiter for Azure API Gateway

Expected flow:

1. **Clarifying questions** (5 min) — scale, workload patterns, target latency.
2. **Iterative high-level design** (15 min) — start simple, add complexity only as motivated.
3. **Deep dive on one component** (20 min) — interviewer drills into one piece. Common targets: storage schema, sharding, consistency model, hot-key mitigation.
4. **Tradeoffs** (10 min) — alternatives, what you traded away.
5. **Mid-interview constraint changes** (5–10 min) — interviewer may add a new requirement to test adaptability.

Critical Microsoft signal: **know everything you mention.** If you say "JWT," expect to explain how JWT is implemented, signature validation, expiry handling, what happens on key rotation. Senior interviewers explicitly probe this.

<InterviewRound name="Behavioral (woven through technical rounds)" duration="varies" format="Embedded in technical rounds · predetermined focus areas" weight="signal">

L63 has **no standalone behavioral round** — that comes at L64. Instead, each interviewer is assigned a behavioral focus area and probes it during their technical round. The recruiter usually tells you which areas which round will cover.

Senior themes:

- **Cross-team mentorship** — coaching mid-level engineers to senior, with named individuals
- **Technical leadership without authority** — driving decisions across teams
- **Strategic judgment** — picking what NOT to build, prioritizing under constraints
- **Growth mindset** — feedback you received and changed your behavior on
- **Conflict resolution** — disagreements with senior engineers or partners
- **Customer obsession** — choosing user-aligned tradeoffs over engineering preference

Stories should land at the **multi-quarter, cross-team** scope. Single-sprint stories read as L62.

</InterviewRound>

## As-Appropriate {#as-appropriate}

The As-Ap round at L63 is invitation-only and signals strong earlier performance. It\'s typically:

- Conducted by the hiring manager or a partner-level engineer
- **Project-focused, not technical testing** — they walk you through the team\'s work and probe fit
- An opportunity for them to **sell you on the team**, especially if multiple teams are competing for senior candidates
- The hiring manager and the As-Ap interviewer **jointly make the final hire decision**

If you reach As-Ap, the offer is likely. The round is more about confirming fit and team alignment than gating you out.

## Preparation plan {#preparation-plan}

System design dominates L63 prep. Coding is the floor.

| Week | Focus | Daily commitment | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | LC mediums + medium-hards (Microsoft-tagged) | 2 mediums/day | 14 mediums solved cleanly |
| 2 | Selective LC hards (Microsoft-tagged) + LLD warm-up | 1 hard + 1 LLD/day | LC 273, 297, 146, 210 solved |
| 3 | LLD design reps (logger, parking lot, scheduler, cache) | 1 LLD design/day | 5 LLDs with implementation-ready code |
| 4 | System design fundamentals (storage, replication, sharding, consistency) | 1 design walkthrough/day | 5 designs from first principles |
| 5 | System design — Microsoft-product flavored (OneDrive, Teams, Outlook, Azure) | 1 mock design/day | 5 product designs at L63 depth |
| 6 | Behavioral story building (cross-team mentorship, leadership) + final mocks | 30 min stories + 1 mock loop | 6 STAR stories at L63 scope, 1 full mock |

:::tip[L63-specific gotchas]
**"Know everything you mention."** Senior Microsoft interviewers will probe any technology you name. If you mention Kafka, expect questions on partition assignment and exactly-once semantics. If you mention JWT, expect questions on signature validation and key rotation. Audit your vocabulary before the interview — only name things you can defend 3 levels deep.

**Adaptability matters.** Microsoft system design interviews often add constraints mid-discussion ("now imagine the latency requirement is 10ms instead of 100ms"). Practice adapting your design rather than restarting it.
:::
