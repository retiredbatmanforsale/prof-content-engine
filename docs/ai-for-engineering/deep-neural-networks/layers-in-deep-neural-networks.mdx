In **Lesson 1**, one **neuron** was a single **linear score** plus an **activation**. A **deep neural network** is what you get when you **stack** many neurons **in layers**, **vertically**: raw **inputs** enter at the **bottom** (or **top**, depending on the diagram—here we draw **input → hidden → output** left to right), **hidden** layers build **intermediate** representations, and the **output** layer produces **predictions**. Researchers including **Geoffrey Hinton** helped popularize the view that **depth** lets a network learn **distributed**, **hierarchical** features—**simple** patterns in early layers, **composed** patterns deeper in—without hand-crafting rules.

This lesson is about **fully connected** (**dense**) layers: **every** neuron in one layer connects to **every** neuron in the next.

## Forward pass (one step at a time, gently)

Write **h⁽⁰⁾ = x** for the input. Layer **ℓ = 1 … L** does: **linear** transform, then **activation** **f** (same idea as Lesson 1, but now **vector** outputs):

$$
\mathbf{z}^{(\ell)} = W^{(\ell)} \mathbf{h}^{(\ell-1)} + \mathbf{b}^{(\ell)}, \qquad
\mathbf{h}^{(\ell)} = f\bigl(\mathbf{z}^{(\ell)}\bigr)
$$

The **output layer** might use **f** = identity (regression) or **softmax** on logits (classification). **Hidden** layers almost always use **ReLU**, **GELU**, **tanh**, etc.

---

## Where **weights** and **biases** live

**Between** layer **ℓ − 1** and **ℓ**:

- **W⁽ℓ⁾** — matrix with one **column** per neuron in layer **ℓ** (or the transpose convention in code—same parameter count).
- **b⁽ℓ⁾** — one bias **per** neuron in layer **ℓ**.

So **parameters** sit on **edges** between layers, not “inside” the circles—the circles are **values** flowing through the net.

---

## Counting parameters (toy example)

If layer **ℓ − 1** has **n_in** neurons and layer **ℓ** has **n_out** neurons:

$$
\text{params for this edge} = n_{\text{in}} \times n_{\text{out}} + n_{\text{out}}
$$

(weights + biases).

## Complexity: what **depth** and **width** buy you

  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-indigo-200 bg-indigo-50/85 p-4 dark:border-indigo-800 dark:bg-indigo-950/30">
    <p className="m-0 text-xs font-bold uppercase text-indigo-800 dark:text-indigo-200">More neurons per layer (width)</p>
    <p className="mt-2 mb-0 text-sm text-slate-800 dark:text-slate-200">More **linear combinations** available **at once**—richer slice of functions at **each** step.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50/85 p-4 dark:border-amber-800 dark:bg-amber-950/30">
    <p className="m-0 text-xs font-bold uppercase text-amber-800 dark:text-amber-200">More hidden layers (depth)</p>
    <p className="mt-2 mb-0 text-sm text-slate-800 dark:text-slate-200">**Compose** nonlinearities—**features of features**. This is the heart of **deep** learning’s **hierarchy** story.</p>
  </div>

**Tradeoff:** more parameters → **more flexible** but **easier to overfit** and **costlier** to train—**architecture** is a design choice.

---

## Nonlinearity **between** layers

If you **only** stacked **linear** maps, the whole network would **collapse** to **one** linear map. **f** **between** layers **breaks** that: each hidden block can **bend** the space so later layers see **new** directions. **No** nonlinearity in hidden layers → **no** extra **expressive** power from depth (for standard fully connected nets).

## Connectionist story (Hinton’s angle, in one breath)

**Geoffrey Hinton** and colleagues pushed the idea that **intelligence** can emerge from **many simple units** adjusting **weights** from **data**—**layers** let those units build **abstractions** without a human listing every rule. You are not memorizing a formula; you are seeing **why** the **stack** is shaped like a **column** of **transformations** from **input** to **output**.

---

## Takeaway

| Idea | One line |
| ---- | -------- |
| **Input layer** | **Features** you provide |
| **Hidden layers** | **Learned** internal vectors; **W, b** **between** layers |
| **Output layer** | **Task** output + final **linear** (and maybe **softmax**) |
| **Dense** | **Full** connectivity **layer to layer** |
| **Parameters** | Mostly **n_in × n_out + n_out** **per** affine block |
| **Depth / width** | **Complexity** and **cost** knobs |
