## The three-layer picture (one hidden layer)

For **binary classification**, the usual pattern is:

1. **Input layer** — one **unit per feature** (the **raw** **x**; **not** learned).  
2. **Hidden layer** — **fully connected** **affine** map + **nonlinearity** (Lesson 2–3). **Combines** features into **learned** **directions**.  
3. **Output layer** — for **two** classes, **one** **logit** **z** plus **sigmoid** **σ** gives **ŷ = P(class 1)** in **(0, 1)** (Lessons 3–4).

Think of **spam vs not spam**: **input** = measured signals (word counts, links, …); **hidden** = internal **combinations** of those signals; **output** = **probability** of **spam**.

**Depth:** **one** hidden layer here = **three** **named** **layers** (**input**, **hidden**, **output**). You can add **more** hidden layers (Lesson 2); the **story** is the same: **linear** blocks and **nonlinearities** chained together.

## Forward pass (compact)

Let $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^d$ be one example. **Trainable** weights and biases (Lesson 7):

- **W₁** is an $h \times d$ matrix, **b₁** $\in \mathbb{R}^h$ — input → hidden  
- **w₂** $\in \mathbb{R}^h$ (as a column vector), **b₂** $\in \mathbb{R}$ — hidden → logit  

**Hidden** pre-activation and activation (hidden units often use **ReLU** in practice; **sigmoid** in the hidden layer is also possible and appeared in the small worked net of Lessons 5–6):

$$
\mathbf{z}^{(1)} = W_1 \mathbf{x} + \mathbf{b}_1, \qquad
\mathbf{a} = \mathrm{ReLU}\bigl(\mathbf{z}^{(1)}\bigr)
$$

**Output** logit and **probability** for **class 1**:

$$
z^{(2)} = \mathbf{w}_2^\top \mathbf{a} + b_2, \qquad
\hat{y} = \sigma\bigl(z^{(2)}\bigr) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-z^{(2)}}}.
$$

That is the **forward pass** (Lesson 5): same computation as **`forward`** / **`predict`** at inference, without updating weights.

## Loss and label (binary classification)

For a **label** **t ∈ {0, 1}**, **binary cross-entropy** (Lesson 4) is the standard pairing with **sigmoid**:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{BCE}} = -\,\bigl( t \log \hat{y} + (1-t)\log(1-\hat{y}) \bigr).
$$

**Training** minimizes **average** **loss** over many **(x, t)** pairs. **Decision rule** on new **x**: e.g. predict **class 1** if **ŷ > 0.5** (threshold can be tuned for precision/recall).

**Alternative head:** two logits + **softmax** for the two classes is equivalent in spirit; the **single** **sigmoid** **output** is the **minimal** **diagram** for **binary** work.

---

## Learning loop (Lessons 6–8 in one breath)

1. **Forward** — compute **ŷ** from **x** and current parameters.  
2. **Loss** — compare **ŷ** to **t**.  
3. **Backward** — **chain rule** through the graph; **gradients** for **W₁**, **b₁**, **w₂**, **b₂**.  
4. **Update** — **θ ← θ − η ∇θ L** (gradient descent).  
5. **Watch** **validation** — if **train** improves but **val** does not, think **overfitting** (Lesson 8): **data**, **model**, **training**, **pre-training**, **post-training** levers.

**Hyperparameters** (**η**, **h**, **d** via feature engineering, **regularization**, **epochs**, …) sit **outside** the tensors updated by **backprop** (Lesson 7).

## One-line story

Binary classification with one hidden layer: **features** in, a **nonlinear hidden layer** mixes them, **sigmoid** outputs **ŷ**; **BCE** trains the stack; **backprop** updates **W₁**, **b₁**, **w₂**, **b₂**; Lessons 7–8 remind you that **hyperparameters** and **data** shape **generalization**.
