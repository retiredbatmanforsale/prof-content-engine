<LessonHero number="5" title="How neural networks make predictions" subtitle="A concrete 3тЖТ4тЖТ2 network: matrix math, sigmoid, and model.predict in code" />

After **loss** (Lesson 4), the natural question is: **where** do the predictions **┼╖** come from? The **forward pass** is the answer: start from **input** **x**, apply each layer in order (**linear** тЖТ **activation**), and end with **outputs**. In libraries, the same computation is what runs inside **`model.predict(...)`** (or **`forward`** in training mode without dropout noise)тАФ**no weight updates**, just **math** from **input** to **output**.

This lesson uses one tiny architecture so you can **read** every symbol:

- **Input layer:** **3** neurons тЖТ feature vector **(xтВБ, xтВВ, xтВГ)**
- **One hidden layer:** **4** neurons тЖТ after sigmoid **(aтВБ, aтВВ, aтВГ, aтВД)**
- **Output layer:** **2** neurons тЖТ after sigmoid **(yтВБ, yтВВ)**

**Parameters:** **WтВБ**, **bтВБ** (input тЖТ hidden), **WтВВ**, **bтВВ** (hidden тЖТ output). Activations use **sigmoid** **╧Г** everywhere here so each value stays in **(0, 1)**тАФgood for **probability-style** heads (you could swap **ReLU** in the hidden layer in practice; the **forward** **recipe** stays the same).

Row **j** of **WтВБ** mixes **x** into pre-activation **zтВБт▒╝**; row **k** of **WтВВ** mixes **a** into **zтВВтВЦ**.

---

## Step 1 тАФ Hidden layer (linear, then sigmoid)

**Pre-activations** (one number per hidden neuron):

$$
\mathbf{z}^{(1)} = W_1 \mathbf{x} + \mathbf{b}_1
$$

**Activations** (apply **╧Г** to **each** component):

$$
a_j = \sigma\bigl(z^{(1)}_j\bigr), \quad j = 1,2,3,4
$$

**Expanded** (nested in the inputs)тАФneuron **j** of the hidden layer:

$$
a_j = \sigma\Bigl( (W_1)_{j1}\, x_1 + (W_1)_{j2}\, x_2 + (W_1)_{j3}\, x_3 + (b_1)_j \Bigr)
$$

So **aтВБ, aтВВ, aтВГ, aтВД** are **four** separate sigmoids of **four** weighted sums of **xтВБ, xтВВ, xтВГ**.

---

## Step 2 тАФ Output layer (linear on **a**, then sigmoid)

$$
\mathbf{z}^{(2)} = W_2 \mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}_2, \qquad
y_k = \sigma\bigl(z^{(2)}_k\bigr), \quad k = 1, 2
$$

**Fully nested** through **x** (substitute each **aт▒╝**):

$$
y_1 = \sigma\Biggl(
\sum_{j=1}^{4} (W_2)_{1j}\,
\sigma\Bigl( \sum_{i=1}^{3} (W_1)_{ji}\, x_i + (b_1)_j \Bigr)
+ (b_2)_1
\Biggr)
$$

$$
y_2 = \sigma\Biggl(
\sum_{j=1}^{4} (W_2)_{2j}\,
\sigma\Bigl( \sum_{i=1}^{3} (W_1)_{ji}\, x_i + (b_1)_j \Bigr)
+ (b_2)_2
\Biggr)
$$

That is the **whole** forward map **(xтВБ, xтВВ, xтВГ) тЖж (yтВБ, yтВВ)** in one glance: **nested** **╧Г** of **affine** functionsтАФ**composition** is what makes the net **nonlinear**.

---

## What **╧Г** does here

$$
\sigma(z) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-z}}
$$

So **yтВБ** and **yтВВ** each live in **(0, 1)**. If this head is **binary** or **multi-label** **probability**, you might pair them with **BCE** (Lesson 4); if you need **two** logits that **compete** (single class out of two), you would often use **one** linear layer + **softmax** insteadтАФsame **forward** **recipe**, different **last** nonlinearity.

---

## Forward pass = **`predict`** in code

| Phase | What runs |
| ----- | --------- |
| **Training** | Forward pass тЖТ **loss** тЖТ **backward** тЖТ **weight** update |
| **Inference** | Forward pass **only** тЖТ return **y** |

So **`model.predict(x)`** (or **`forward`** with **no_grad**) is **exactly** the chain above: **multiply**, **add**, **╧Г**, repeat. **No** gradients required to **use** a trained modelтАФonly to **learn** **WтВБ, bтВБ, WтВВ, bтВВ**.

---

## One-line story

**Input** тЖТ **WтВБx + bтВБ** тЖТ **╧Г** тЖТ **a** тЖТ **WтВВa + bтВВ** тЖТ **╧Г** тЖТ **y**. That is **prediction**.
